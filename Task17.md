## What is Virtual Reality?
**Virtual reality (VR)** is a technology that creates virtual environments and experiences using a mixture of hardware and software. It surrounds your senses especially sight and sound and tricks the brain into feeling like you’re inside a virtual space. It blends the visual, audio, and motion tracking techniques to simulate real or imaginary environments, making the experience immersive and realistic. 
These experiences are used both professionally (training, education, and collaboration) and personally (video gaming, TV, entertainment).
One of the biggest advantages of VR is that it allows people to experience situations that might be difficult, expensive, or even dangerous in the real world.

## Technology 
- **HEADSET**: The VR headset is the gateway between the physical and the virtual worlds. It uses twin high-definition screens and tracking sensors to simulate immersive VR environments. Though the current generation of VR gear is still dominated by the Oculus Rift and HTC Vive, new and advanced options are emerging each year.
- **COMPUTER**: Immersive and interactive VR environments require significant graphical and computational horsepower. VR tricks the mind into accepting the virtual world as real, but insufficient power can cause stuttering and create a disconnect between the real and the virtual.
- **SOFTWARE**: Once the headset is up and running, software programs are added to support the intended service offerings. VR’s software market is dominated by video games, but educational programs are creating a niche. Health sciences VR programs fall into two broad categories: anatomical education using generic models and clinical case preparation using patient-specific renderings.

## Difference between Augmented reality (AR) and Virtual reality (VR)
---
**Virtual reality**
VR immerses the participant into a 100% simulated environment. Inside the headset, they see only a virtual scene.

**Augmented reality**
AR literally augments reality. It overlays what the participant sees in the real world with computer-generated content that’s interactive and engaging.
<br>It blends digital objects with what we see in our surroundings.

|Aspect| Virtual reality | Augmented reality |
|----------------------|--|--
|      Environment                |Completely virtual  | Surroundings + digital enhancements
|User Experience| Shut off from reality | Still connected with reality|
